Seven recipes, carried from Thailand and cooked the slow way. A few we are known for.

★ Most-loved

Masaman
Chicken Curry

A curry that travelled the spice roads. Masaman is gentle and deep — cardamom, cinnamon and clove folded through coconut, chicken slow-cooked until it falls apart, soft potato and a scatter of roasted peanuts. Sweet, savoury, barely warm. The dish our guests drive back for.

01
To begin

Mixed
Starter Platter

The opening act. Golden spring rolls, chicken satay smoke-kissed over the grill, prawn toast and delicate parcels — arranged around hand-carved garnishes with three dipping sauces. Built to be shared, slowly, over the first pour.

02
From the iron

Slow-cooked
Sizzling Beef

Beef braised long and low until it gives way, then brought to the table still singing on the iron. Garlic, black pepper and dark caramelised soy, the edges crisp, the centre tender. You hear it before you taste it.

03
The bright one

Thai
Green Curry

Brighter, bolder, hotter. Fresh green chillies pounded with lemongrass and galangal, loosened with coconut cream and lifted by Thai basil and the citrus snap of kaffir lime. Fragrant heat that builds and lingers.

04
Hot & sour

Tom Yum
Soup

Thailand in a bowl. A clear, fiery broth sharpened with lemongrass, lime leaf and galangal, plump mushrooms and prawns, a slick of chilli oil drawn across the top. Hot, sour and aromatic — the soup that wakes the whole table.

05
Wok-charred

Pad Kee Mao
Drunken Noodles

Wide rice noodles seared hard in the wok and tumbled with chilli, garlic, holy basil and crisp greens. Smoky from the flame — the cook's wok hei — with a slow chilli heat that arrives just behind the sweetness.

06

The Room

Step inside and Hertford slips away. Teak tables and antique timber, carried piece by piece from Thailand, sit beneath hand-carved temple reliefs and the soft glow of brass lanterns. Intimate, candlelit, unhurried — a room made for long evenings and good company.
